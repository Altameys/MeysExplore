CHINA

THE GREAT WALL










The Great Wall of China, an enduring symbol of Chinese civilization, spans over 13,000 miles across northern China. Originally constructed during the Qin Dynasty over 2,000 years ago and further expanded during the Ming Dynasty, its primary purpose was defensive, serving as a barrier against nomadic tribes and military threats from the north. Comprising various materials such as stone, brick, and earth, the wall is not a single continuous structure but rather a series of interconnected walls, watchtowers, and fortifications built across diverse terrain. Despite its historic defensive role, today it is celebrated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, attracting millions of visitors annually to marvel at its architectural grandeur and cultural significance.
